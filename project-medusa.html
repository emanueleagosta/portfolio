Medusa is a macOS app that makes the cursor easier to see in any context, from live coding sessions to everyday work. It adds subtle visual enhancements around the pointer so movements and clicks are always clear without feeling distracting.

Made for people who work on screen.

Medusa is designed for presenters, educators, live coders, tutorial creators and anyone who spends time sharing their screen. It helps audiences follow along, improves clarity in recordings and supports users who benefit from stronger visual focus on the cursor.

Features

Aura effects that highlight the cursor.

Soft rings surround the cursor to gently increase its presence, helping viewers track movements even on busy backgrounds.

Dynamic trails that clarify motion.

Smooth, customizable trails make it easy to follow fast cursor movements during presentations, live coding or screen sharing.

Adaptive contrast for any background.

Medusa intelligently boosts cursor contrast so it remains visible whether the background is light, dark or highly detailed.

Design Process

Medusa started from a practical need: during workshops and live coding sessions, the cursor was easy to lose against complex interfaces and screens. The goal was to create a visibility tool that felt native to macOS and respectful of the overall UI.

The team explored different visual treatments for auras, trails and click highlights, iterating until the effects were strong enough for large audiences yet subtle enough for everyday use. Adaptive contrast was introduced to keep the cursor readable across light, dark and high‚Äëcontrast backgrounds without manual adjustment.

Performance was a constant constraint. Medusa was optimized to remain lightweight and efficient so it can run continuously in the background, enhancing visibility without impacting system responsiveness.
