B.Roll is a movies and TV shows tracker that focuses on the essentials: building and managing your watchlist, seeing what to watch next, and discovering new titles in a calm, familiar interface.

Designed for iOS and iPadOS, the app feels intuitive from the first launch.

Made for movies and TV shows lovers.

B.Roll is used by movie lovers, binge watchers, and casual viewers who want a better way to organize what they watch without the noise. Whether you are keeping track of ongoing TV shows, building a queue for movie night, or discovering what is trending, B.Roll keeps the experience simple and focused.

Features

All the info you need.

Each movie and TV show has a clear page with artwork, description, episodes, where to watch, cast and crew and trailers. Adding or removing it from your list is one tap away.

Know what to watch next.

A dedicated Watch Next view shows the next episode for every series you are following. Open the app and you immediately see where you can continue.

Search instantly.

Find specific movies and TV shows or simply browse by genre. You can focus results on streaming services you already use, so suggestions match the subscriptions you pay for.

See your watchlist without unlocking your phone.

Widgets and Lock Screen widgets show favorite posters, upcoming movies from your list and the next episodes to watch at a glance. B.Roll keeps your watchlist visible even when the app is closed.

Use Cases

Most movie and TV tracking apps try to do everything at once, mixing social feeds, cluttered discovery, heavy onboarding, and account creation before you can add a single title. B.Roll strips this down to what matters: a clear watchlist, fast discovery, all privately synced with iCloud, without accounts or tracking.

Design Process

B.Roll evolved through several iterations, each focused on reducing friction and aligning more closely with Apple's design language.

Research included deconstructing successful apps of the same category like TV Time, Must, Letterboxd, to understand clean search experiences, discovery and simplified navigation. These insights shaped the current structure: three core tabs (Explore, Movies and TV Shows plus Search) with clear hierarchy.

The current version adopts liquid glass design system, making the app feel native on iOS and iPadOS.
