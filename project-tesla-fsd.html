At Tesla, I helped envision and design the learning architecture that scales internal training for complex ADAS features. The Challenge

Prior to establishing a unified content system, internal training materials on advanced features like Autosteer on City Streets and Traffic Light and Stop Sign Control were often fragmented. Content was being created ad-hoc, leading to several critical business friction points:

  • Messaging Alignment: A critical need existed to standardize safety messaging. It was imperative to uniformly communicate that FSD remains an SAE Level 2 system requiring active driver supervision, actively mitigating complacency risk across all materials.
  • Content Velocity: The lack of reusable components meant that every time a new software version dropped, instructional content had to be built from scratch, causing bottlenecks.
  • Cognitive Overload: Internal onboarding materials were frequently too dense, over-indexing on technical jargon rather than actionable learning outcomes.

Mapping the fragmented state of legacy instructional content across internal systems.

Methodology & Governance

To solve the bottleneck, I proposed shifting from individual course creation to a systemic Content Design System. This required establishing strong governance and cross-functional alignment.

Adopting a "Do Less" Approach: By stripping away extraneous cognitive load, we focused on core learning experiences. We developed strict taxonomies that simplified complex engineering jargon—such as neural network heuristics—without sacrificing technical accuracy.

Cross-Functional Collaboration: Partnering directly with engineering Subject Matter Experts (SMEs) and safety teams was crucial. We audited the entire learning flow to ensure that every module accurately reflected the critical shift to the "(Supervised)" terminology and its accompanying legal and safety implications.

Abstracted wireframes illustrating the new modular learning taxonomy.

The Solution

The resulting deliverable was a standardized, modular framework for all FSD (Supervised) L&D materials, hosted on proprietary internal authoring tools.

Instead of building bespoke courses, the team now had access to reusable learning templates. For example, when rolling out an update for Traffic Light and Stop Sign Control, instructional designers could pull pre-approved content blocks that automatically enforced consistent voice, tone, and the mandatory "Active supervision required" safety disclaimers.

This architecture allowed the L&D team to rapidly assemble, rather than write, new training flows. It abstracted the UI layer for content authors, allowing them to focus entirely on pedagogy.

Impact & Learnings

The implementation of the Content Design System fundamentally transformed how Tesla scales internal knowledge for ADAS features.

  • Scalability: Drastically reduced the time-to-market required to roll out new instructional modules following consecutive FSD software updates.
  • 100% Consistency: Achieved total alignment on safety messaging across all internal training touchpoints, ensuring every employee understands the paramount importance of driver responsibility.

Key Learnings

This project underscored the critical importance of building flexible, component-based content systems when operating in rapidly evolving regulatory environments. By decoupling the learning structure from the raw engineering data, we created an ecosystem that could adapt as fast as the software it was meant to teach.
